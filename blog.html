The Physiology of Heat & Cold

Contrast therapy is more than just a luxury experience; it is a powerful biological reset. When you enter the sauna, your body undergoes vasodilation—the widening of blood vessels—which increases blood flow and delivers oxygen-rich nutrients to fatigued muscles.

The subsequent cold plunge triggers vasoconstriction, effectively flushing out metabolic waste and reducing cellular inflammation. This "vascular pumping" action strengthens the circulatory system and forces the body to adapt to extreme environments, building both physical and mental resilience.

Wellness FAQ

Common questions about recovery, contrast therapy, and wellness protocols.

How often should I practice contrast therapy?
For most people, 2-3 sessions per week provide significant benefits. Professional athletes may use it daily during heavy training periods. Always listen to your body and consult with a professional if you're new to extreme temperatures.
What is the ideal temperature for a cold plunge?
Most health benefits are observed between 39°F and 50°F (4°C - 10°C). Beginners should start at the higher end and gradually lower the temperature as they build tolerance.
How long should I stay in the sauna?
A standard session lasts 15-20 minutes at 170°F-200°F. If you're doing contrast therapy, we recommend 15 minutes of heat followed by 2-5 minutes of cold, repeated for 3 rounds.
